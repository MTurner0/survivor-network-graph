No season is an island: The many returning contestants of Survivor

42 seasons of the American reality television show Survivor have aired, and contestants have been returning since season 8 (in 2004). In this graph, vertices represent seasons. To see a season's name, simply brush over the graph (i.e. drag your cursor or your finger across the screen). An edge (or line) between two seasons indicates that at least one contestant played on both seasons. The thicker the edge, the more contestants played on both seasons.

There are four types of seasons in terms of newbie/returner composition:

  • All newbie seasons, where all contestants are on the show for their first time;
  • All returner seasons, where all contestants are playing for at least their second time;
  • Half and half seasons, where half of the contestants are returning and the other half are first-time players.
  • Of the three seasons with this premise, two were themed as "Fans v. Favorites" (pitting a tribe of returning players against a tribe of unrelated first-time players) and one was "Blood v. Water" (pitting a tribe of returning players against their loved ones, who were playing for their first time).
  • Captains seasons, where the majority of the contestants are first-time players, with each tribe having a returning player (the so-called "captain").

    • Data from Dan Oehm, processed in R.
